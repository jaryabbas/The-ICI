Five Roots of Religion (Usul ad-Din)

1. Tawhid (Oneness of God)

Belief in the absolute oneness and uniqueness of God.

2. Adalah (Justice of God)

Belief that God is just and never commits any injustice.

3. Nubuwwah (Prophethood)

Belief in the necessity of divinely appointed prophets.

4. Imamah (Leadership)

Belief in the leadership of the divinely appointed Imams.

5. Ma'ad (Day of Resurrection)

Belief in life after death and final judgment.

Ten Branches of Religion (Furu' ad-Din)

1. Salat (Prayer)

Performing five daily prayers.

2. Sawm (Fasting)

Fasting during the month of Ramadan.

3. Hajj (Pilgrimage)

Pilgrimage to Mecca for those who are able.

4. Zakat (Almsgiving)

Giving a portion of wealth to those in need.

5. Khums (One-Fifth Tax)

Giving 20% of savings for religious and charitable purposes.

6. Jihad (Struggle)

Striving for righteousness and justice.

7. Amr-bil-Ma’ruf (Enjoining Good)

Encouraging moral and virtuous behavior.

8. Nahi Anil Munkar (Forbidding Evil)

Preventing immoral and sinful acts.

9. Tawalla (Loving the Righteous)

Having affection for those close to God.

10. Tabarra (Dissociation from Evil)

Distancing from those who oppose righteousness.

The Five Roots of Religion

1. Oneness of God (Tawhid)

Say, "He is Allah—One; Allah—the Sustainer.
He has never had offspring, nor was He born.
And there is none comparable to Him."
Quran, Chapter, 112

Tawhid is the religion's central and single most important concept, upon which a Muslim's entire religious adherence rests.

God, according to Islam, is a universal God, rather than a local, tribal, or parochial one, and is an absolute who integrates all affirmative values.

Duties which follow after believing in One God, is believing in the Oneness of Universal System that all creation of this universe is of a single creator and
Humanity is One irrespective of religion, nationality, etc. and we have to honor, care & love all creations, as they are of one own’s God.

2. Justice of God (Adal)

“Allah is always standing firm on justice.
There is no Allah but Him, the Mighty, the Wise.”
Quran, Chapter, 3:18

Anyone who believes in Islamic monotheism must also believe in God's justice. God is just, and He never wrongs. Moreover, He does not have a cause for injustice toward His creatures, for an injustice is an immoral action; and God is incapable of evil. God is omniscient and never neglects any matter. God is self-sufficient, and He is not in need of the help of people. God is the possessor of everything, and His wisdom transcends the universe.

The question of human's predestination or freedom of choice has preoccupied humankind throughout the ages. Compulsionist, holds that human beings do not have the freedom of choice. Every decision, utterance, and action people make has already been predestined since the time of creation. But the Free Will, declares that human beings are masters of their own acts. This notion is in concordance with the Quran which states,

“None will be wronged in anything, nor will you be requited anything except that which you used to do” (36:54).
“Whatever good reaches you is from Allah, but whatever evil befalls you is from yourself” (4:79).

The action-reaction law is applicable but the end result is on God, if He will, He can change the result. Thus, we are in middle of Compulsion (fate) and Free Will in this world.
And Justice will be served on the Day of Judgement if anyone has suffered in this world by the unjust person.

3. Prophethood (Nabuat)

And We did not send any messenger except [speaking] in the language of
his people to state clearly our message to them. God guides or
causes to go astray whomever He wants. God is Majestic and All-wise.
Quran, Chapter, 14:4

Since, the advent of humanity, God provided all the necessities for humans even before their creation.
And guidance was also the essential part to be on the God’s path which is embedded in the human’s nature.
Thus, God send its infallible representative as teacher & masters in form of Prophets.

In total, God send down 1,24,000 Prophets (or Messengers) in this world for our guidance starting from first human, Prophet Adam and the last, Prophet Muhammad [s].

"Then We sent our messengers one after another." (Quran 23:44)

In between some of great Prophets came like Abraham, Noah, Solomon, Moses, Jesus etc.
and all Prophets revive the previous Prophet laws and have scrolls or books from God’s revelation.

Muslims hold the Quran, as it was revealed to Muhammad[s], to be God's final revelation to humankind,
and therefore a completion and confirmation of previous scriptures, such as the Bible (Injeel),
Torah (or Tawrat) , Psalms (or Zabur).

4. Guardianship (Imamate)

“Verily I make thee Imam for humankind; ‘(Abraham) said “And of my offspring”? He said: “My covenant reach not the unjust”.
Quran, Chapter, 2:124

Imams are the spiritual and political successors to the Holy Prophet Muhammad[s] and are exemplary human individuals who not only rule over the Muslim community with justice, but are also able to preserve and interpret the Islamic law ( or shariat) and the esoteric meaning of the Qur'an.

“On the judgement day when We will call every nation with their Imams.” -Quran, 17:71

"Whoever dies without knowing the Imam of his time dies the death of ignorance (Jāhiliyyah)."
-Holy Prophet Mohammad[s] (Sahih Muslim, Hadith 1851)

Imams are from Allah, through Prophet Muhammad[s].
Imamate is a divinely-appointed status; it is beyond the jurisdiction of the community.

"O you who have believed, obey Allāh and obey the Messenger and those in authority among you. And if you disagree over anything, refer it to Allāh and the Messenger...” (Quran 4:59)

The Holy Prophet Mohammad[s] said,
"O people, I am only a human being and I am about to respond to the messenger of my Lord [i.e. the call of death]. I am leaving behind two precious things among you.
The first of the two is the Book of Allah, in it is guidance and light. So get hold of the Quran and adhere to it.
And my Ahlul Bayt (Household). I urge you to remember God regarding my Ahlul Bayt..."
(Sahih Muslim, part 7, Kitab fada'il al-­Sahabah [Maktabat wa Matba’at Muhammad ‘Ali Subayh wa Awladuhu: Cairo] pp. 122-123.)

Hence, the words and deeds (or sunnah) of Prophet Muhammad[s] and the 12 Imams are a guide and model for the Muslim community to follow;

"Islam will not end until there have been Twelve Caliphs.”
- Holy Prophet Muhammad[s] (Sahih Bukhaari (no. 7222); Sahih Muslim (no. 1821))


Just as every Prophet is infallible, so too are Lady Fatima Zahra[s] and the Twelve Imams are free from error and sin from God’s protection, a doctrine known as Ismah (or infallibility).
Ismah is essential to uphold the authenticity of divine law and ensure the guide's absolute credibility.

5. Day ofJudgement and Resurrection (Qayamat)

"Verily We shall give life to the dead, and We record all that they send before and that they leave behind, and We have taken account of all things in a clear Book [of evidence]."
(Quran, 36:12)

And to every soul will be paid in full of its deeds, and He knows best all that they do.
(Quran, 39:70)

The day of resurrection will be the time when all of humankind will be brought back to their original physical form.
Every human being, from the past to the present, will be resurrected and examined by their deeds.
This day is called the “Day of Judgment.”
Human being does not end at death, but, in fact, continues afterward towards a new life.
The Quran calls this life the “true life.”

Many people wonder if eventually humankind is going to die out, why then were people created in the first place?
Humankind was created to be the caretaker of the earth; thus, life on earth becomes a test, to maintain and preserve nature. More importantly, to maintain and preserve our moral natures.

According to Islam, a person will be judged according to his or her life's actions.
People whose deeds and actions in life are mainly good will enter Heaven (or Paradise).
However, if one's deeds or actions are mainly evil, he or she will enter Hell.
On the Day of Judgment, everyone will know the fate of everyone else. Both Heaven and Hell have different levels.
The worst of people will be in the lowest depths of Hell, while the best of people will be in the highest parts of Heaven.



The Ten Branches of Religion

1. Prayer (Salah)

"Indeed, prayer prohibits immorality and wrongdoing, and the remembrance of Allah is greater."
Quran, 29:45

Muslims must perform five daily prayers as an obligation to connect with Allah and seek His guidance.

2. Fasting (Sawm)

"O you who have believed, fasting has been prescribed upon you as it was upon those before you that you may attain piety."
Quran, 2:183

During the month of Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn to sunset as an act of self-purification and devotion.

3. Charity (Zakat)

"Establish prayer and give Zakat, and whatever good you put forward for yourselves—you will find it with Allah."
Quran, 2:110

Muslims are required to give a portion of their wealth to help the needy and purify their income.

4. One-Fifth Tax (Khums)

"Know that whatever you obtain of war booty, then indeed, one-fifth of it is for Allah and the Messenger."
Quran, 8:41

Khums is a 20% tax on surplus earnings to be given to religious scholars and the needy.

5. Pilgrimage (Hajj)

"And complete the Hajj and Umrah for Allah."
Quran, 2:196

Every Muslim must perform Hajj at least once in their lifetime if they are physically and financially able.

6. Struggle in the Way of Allah (Jihad)

"And strive for the cause of Allah with the striving due to Him."
Quran, 22:78

Jihad refers to both the internal struggle against sin and the defense of justice and faith.

7. Enjoining Good (Amr bil Maruf)

"Let there be a group among you who call to righteousness, enjoin good, and forbid evil."
Quran, 3:104

Muslims should encourage good actions in society and promote righteousness.

8. Forbidding Evil (Nahi anil Munkar)

"And do not incline toward those who do wrong, lest the Fire should touch you."
Quran, 11:113

It is the duty of Muslims to discourage wrongdoing and prevent injustice.

9. Expressing Love for the Prophet and His Family (Tawalla)

"Say: I do not ask for any reward from you except love for my close relatives."
Quran, 42:23

Muslims must express love and loyalty to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and his Ahlul Bayt (family).

10. Dissociation from Enemies of Islam (Tabarra)

"You will not find a people who believe in Allah and the Last Day
having affection for those who oppose Allah and His Messenger."
Quran, 58:22

Muslims should distance themselves from those who oppose righteousness and justice.
